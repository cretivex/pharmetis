Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 31 March 2026

Company: Phartimetic (India)

Phartimetic operates a B2B pharmaceutical marketplace that connects verified medicine suppliers, pharma vendors, pharmacies, hospitals, and institutional buyers for online medicine wholesale transactions. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect personal and business information when you use our pharmaceutical platform India. By accessing or using Phartimetic, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

1. Marketplace Nature and Scope

Phartimetic is a digital marketplace and technology platform. We are not a manufacturer of medicines and do not produce pharmaceutical products. We provide tools for account creation, catalog management, discovery, order placement, and transaction coordination between buyers and suppliers. We also coordinate logistics data with independent third-party delivery partners where required.

2. Data We Collect

We collect information directly from users, automatically through platform usage, and from lawful third-party sources.

  • Account Data: Name, mobile number for OTP login, email address, business name, GST or tax details (if provided), registration information, and role type (vendor account or buyer account).
  • Business Profile Data: Catalog details, product descriptions, medicine categories, licensing and compliance information uploaded by pharma vendors and medicine suppliers.
  • Order and Transaction Data: Purchase orders, item quantities, quoted prices, invoices, delivery addresses, order status history, and communication logs tied to the order system.
  • Operational Data: Device identifiers, browser details, IP address, usage logs, page interactions, and timestamps for security monitoring and platform optimization.
  • Support Data: Messages, complaints, verification documents, and correspondence with our support team.

3. OTP Login and Authentication Data

Phartimetic uses OTP login to improve account security for vendor accounts and buyer accounts. We may process mobile numbers, OTP validation attempts, login timestamps, and device signals to authenticate users, prevent fraud, and detect unauthorized access. Users must keep their registered contact details current and are responsible for safeguarding their account access credentials and devices.

4. Purpose of Processing

We process data for legitimate and lawful business purposes relevant to a B2B pharmaceutical marketplace:

  • Creating and managing medicine supplier and buyer accounts.
  • Enabling product catalog upload and search for online medicine wholesale procurement.
  • Operating the order system, including confirmations, invoicing records, and status notifications.
  • Sharing required shipment details with third-party delivery partners to execute delivery.
  • Ensuring platform security, fraud prevention, abuse detection, and legal compliance.
  • Customer support, dispute handling, audits, analytics, and product improvements.
  • Communicating policy updates, service notices, and critical account alerts.

5. Legal Basis and Consent

Where required by applicable law, we rely on user consent, contractual necessity, legal obligations, and legitimate business interests. By registering and using Phartimetic, users consent to necessary data processing to facilitate B2B pharmaceutical marketplace operations. Users may withdraw optional consents where permitted, but essential data processing may continue when required for platform functionality, legal obligations, or security.

6. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell personal data. We share data only as necessary for marketplace operations and compliance.

  • Between Marketplace Participants: Buyer details may be shared with suppliers to process orders; supplier details may be shared with buyers for procurement records and verification.
  • Third-Party Delivery Partners: Delivery-related information such as consignee name, address, contact details, and package metadata may be shared to fulfill shipments.
  • Service Providers: Hosting, analytics, communication, anti-fraud, and customer-support vendors may process data under contractual safeguards.
  • Legal and Regulatory Authorities: Data may be disclosed when required by law, court orders, investigations, or statutory requests in India.
  • Corporate Transactions: In case of merger, restructuring, or acquisition, data may be transferred subject to confidentiality and legal safeguards.

7. Data Protection and Security Measures

Phartimetic implements reasonable technical, organizational, and administrative safeguards to protect user and business information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Security controls may include access controls, encryption in transit where applicable, logging, role-based permissions, periodic reviews, and incident response procedures. No digital system is completely risk-free; users should also maintain internal security controls and endpoint protection.

8. Data Retention

We retain account, transactional, and compliance records for as long as necessary to provide services, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, prevent fraud, and meet statutory obligations under Indian law. Retention periods may vary by data category. When data is no longer required, we will delete, anonymize, or securely archive it as appropriate.

9. User Responsibilities for Privacy

  • Provide accurate and up-to-date registration data for vendor accounts and buyer accounts.
  • Use OTP login and platform credentials responsibly; do not share access with unauthorized persons.
  • Upload only lawful business information and avoid unnecessary personal data in catalogs or messages.
  • Ensure data shared with other users or delivery partners is accurate and legally permitted.
  • Promptly report suspected account compromise or unauthorized activity to support.

10. Company Responsibilities for Privacy

  • Operate Phartimetic as a secure and transparent pharmaceutical platform India users can trust.
  • Process data strictly for marketplace operations, legal compliance, and service quality.
  • Maintain contractual controls with relevant processors and third-party service providers.
  • Respond to privacy requests and user queries in a reasonable timeframe.
  • Provide clear policy updates and notice of material privacy changes.

11. Cross-Border Data Processing

Some technical processing or backup infrastructure may involve systems outside your immediate jurisdiction. Where cross-border processing occurs, Phartimetic will use reasonable safeguards and contractual commitments to maintain privacy and security standards consistent with applicable law and business obligations.

12. Children and Restricted Use

Phartimetic is designed for business users and commercial entities in pharmaceutical trade. It is not intended for minors. Accounts must be created by authorized representatives of businesses, pharmacies, hospitals, wholesalers, distributors, or other institutional entities.
