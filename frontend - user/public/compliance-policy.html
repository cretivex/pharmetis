Compliance Policy

Effective Date: 31 March 2026

Company: Phartimetic (India)

This Compliance Policy defines standards and obligations for all participants using Phartimetic, a B2B pharmaceutical marketplace that connects medicine suppliers, pharma vendors, pharmacies, and hospitals for lawful online medicine wholesale activity on a regulated pharmaceutical platform India.

1. Compliance Objective

Phartimetic is committed to promoting lawful, transparent, and responsible pharmaceutical trade. The objective of this policy is to establish a robust compliance framework that supports legal procurement, seller accountability, buyer due diligence, secure data handling, and responsible logistics coordination through third-party delivery partners.

2. Marketplace Status and Regulatory Position

Phartimetic is a digital marketplace and does not manufacture medicines. We are not a manufacturer, formulation owner, or production unit for listed products. Product responsibility, legal marketability, quality assurance, storage compliance, and statutory declarations remain with the respective suppliers and buyers according to applicable laws.

3. Account-Level Compliance Requirements

All users must maintain compliant and verifiable accounts:

  • Vendor Accounts: Must provide accurate business identity, licenses, certifications, and product listing information relevant to lawful pharma trade.
  • Buyer Accounts: Must use valid business credentials and place orders only for authorized institutional use.
  • OTP Login Controls: OTP login is required for authentication integrity. Users must protect account devices and prevent unauthorized OTP access.
  • KYC/Verification: Phartimetic may request supporting documents and perform periodic verification checks.

4. Product Listing and Documentation Compliance

Suppliers must ensure that all listed products are legally tradable and accurately represented.

  • Product names, compositions, dosage forms, strengths, and packaging must be truthful.
  • Claims related to approvals, certifications, and compliance status must be verifiable.
  • No misleading, deceptive, counterfeit, or prohibited medicine listing is allowed.
  • Batch-specific documentation and certificates must be provided where required by law or transaction terms.

5. Buyer Due Diligence Responsibilities

Buyers using the order system must perform adequate due diligence before purchase, including supplier verification, legal authorization checks, product suitability evaluation, and destination compliance checks. Buyers are responsible for ensuring procurement aligns with internal policies, professional standards, and applicable legal requirements.

6. Order System Compliance Controls

The Phartimetic order system creates an auditable transaction trail for B2B pharmaceutical marketplace operations. Users must avoid off-record manipulations and ensure platform records reflect actual commercial intent, product details, quantities, and delivery destinations. Suspicious ordering patterns may trigger additional review, temporary restrictions, or account suspension.

7. Third-Party Delivery Compliance

Delivery is executed by independent third-party delivery partners. Compliance obligations include lawful shipment declarations, accurate consignee details, proper packaging standards, and handling instructions where needed. Users must cooperate with shipping documentation, customs or regional checks, and lawful traceability requirements. Phartimetic may share shipment-related data strictly for fulfillment and legal compliance purposes.

8. Data Protection and Privacy Compliance

Data protection is central to compliance operations. Phartimetic applies administrative and technical safeguards for account data, transaction data, OTP authentication data, and support records. Users must only upload necessary business information and avoid unauthorized sharing of personal data. Data processing is governed by the Privacy Policy and applicable laws in India.

9. Anti-Fraud, Risk Monitoring, and Investigations

Phartimetic may use automated and manual controls to identify suspicious behavior, account abuse, high-risk listing activity, or fraudulent transactions. This may include log analysis, account linkage checks, delivery anomaly detection, and identity verification. Users agree to cooperate with compliance inquiries and provide timely explanations or documents when requested.

10. Prohibited Activities Under this Policy

  • Offering counterfeit or unlawfully sourced medicines.
  • Misstating manufacturer identity, product origin, or regulatory status.
  • Circumventing account verification, OTP controls, or platform safeguards.
  • Submitting fabricated documents, false invoices, or manipulated order data.
  • Using the platform for unlawful distribution, diversion, or unauthorized resale channels.
  • Violating applicable pharmaceutical, anti-corruption, trade, tax, or data laws.

11. Incident Reporting and Corrective Action

Users must promptly report suspected compliance breaches, data incidents, account compromise, or unlawful listing activity. Phartimetic may impose corrective actions including listing removal, temporary holds, enhanced verification, transaction restrictions, or permanent account termination depending on severity and legal risk.

12. Audit Rights and Recordkeeping

Phartimetic reserves the right to conduct internal compliance reviews of account and transaction activity. Users should maintain adequate records of business licenses, invoices, product documentation, and shipment records to support legal and operational audits. Incomplete or inconsistent records may lead to compliance escalations.

13. Roles and Responsibilities

Company Responsibilities:

  • Maintain a transparent and accountable compliance framework for the marketplace.
  • Implement verification, monitoring, and policy enforcement mechanisms.
  • Support lawful data processing and privacy safeguards.
  • Act on credible reports of policy violations in a timely manner.

User Responsibilities:

  • Use the platform only for lawful B2B pharmaceutical transactions.
  • Keep account information current and verification-ready.
  • Ensure listings and orders are accurate, legal, and operationally feasible.
  • Comply with all applicable laws, licenses, and contractual obligations.

14. Enforcement and Penalties

Violations of this Compliance Policy may result in warnings, listing delisting, order cancellation, account suspension, permanent termination, reporting to competent authorities, or legal action as permitted by law. Enforcement decisions may consider severity, recurrence, intent, and cooperation during investigation.

15. Policy Review and Updates

Phartimetic may update this policy to reflect legal developments, risk trends, technology improvements, and business operations in India. Updated versions become effective upon publication unless otherwise specified. Continued use of the platform indicates acceptance of the updated policy framework.

16. Contact and Compliance Support

For compliance concerns, reporting issues, or documentation queries, contact support@phartimetic.com.
